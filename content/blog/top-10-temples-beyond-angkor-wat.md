# Top 10 Temples to Visit Beyond Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat may be the crown jewel, but the Angkor Archaeological Park contains dozens of magnificent temples that deserve your attention. Here are ten that should be on every visitor's list.

## 1. Bayon Temple

At the heart of Angkor Thom, Bayon is famous for its 54 towers featuring 216 smiling stone faces. Built in the late 12th century as the official state temple of King Jayavarman VII, it represents the intersection of Buddhism and Hinduism in Khmer culture.

## 2. Ta Prohm

Known as the "Tomb Raider temple," Ta Prohm's intertwining silk-cotton tree roots create an otherworldly atmosphere. This Buddhist monastery and university offers a powerful lesson in nature's reclamation of human achievement.

## 3. Banteay Srei

Though smaller in scale, Banteay Srei boasts the finest carvings in all of Angkor. Dedicated to Shiva, this 10th-century temple features intricate pink sandstone details that remain remarkably preserved.

## 4. Preah Khan

This sprawling temple complex served as a Buddhist university and city. Its labyrinthine corridors offer hours of exploration, and its blend of Buddhist and Hindu symbolism reflects Cambodia's religious evolution.

## 5. Neak Pean

A unique island temple situated in the center of a baray (reservoir), Neak Pean was designed as a symbolic representation of Anavatapta, a mythical lake in the Himalayas believed to cure illnesses.

## 6. East Mebon

This mountain-temple was built on an artificial island in the Eastern Baray. Its massive elephant statues at the corners and detailed lintels make it particularly photogenic.

## 7. Ta Som

A smaller temple featuring a classic face-tower and a massive strangler fig tree framing its eastern entrance. It's less visited, offering moments of quiet contemplation.

## 8. Banteay Kdei

This sprawling Buddhist monastery features elegant carvings and peaceful courtyards. Its proximity to Ta Prohm makes it an easy addition to any temple circuit.

## 9. Srah Srang

Though technically a royal bathing pond rather than a temple, this 10th-century reservoir offers stunning sunrise views and a peaceful respite from temple exploration.

## 10. Prasat Kravan

This small, elegant temple from 921 AD is noted for its unique brick carvings, including rare representations of Vishnu. Its modest scale makes for a quick but rewarding visit.

Each temple tells a story of Cambodia's rich history, artistic achievement, and spiritual heritage. Taking time to explore beyond Angkor Wat will deepen your understanding and appreciation of this remarkable civilization.
