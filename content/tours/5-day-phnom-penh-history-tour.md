# 5-Day Phnom Penh History Tour

This five-day intensive program focuses on Cambodia's modern history, from the colonial period through the Khmer Rouge era to contemporary rebuilding. Ideal for history and political science students.

## Day 1: Colonial Phnom Penh

**Morning:** Arrival and orientation walk along Sisowath Quay, discussing French urban planning and the Mekong's role in Cambodian history.

**Afternoon:** Visit the National Museum of Cambodia, with focus on Khmer art and the colonial context of museum creation.

**Evening:** Welcome dinner and introduction to Cambodian history.

## Day 2: The Khmer Rouge Era

**Morning:** Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum (S-21), the former school turned prison. Guided by a survivor when possible.

**Afternoon:** Choeung Ek Killing Fields with discussion of the DK regime's rise and fall.

**Evening:** Debrief session with genocide education specialist.

## Day 3: Royal Cambodia

**Morning:** Royal Palace and Silver Pagoda, exploring the continuity of Khmer monarchy through colonial and revolutionary periods.

**Afternoon:** Independence Monument and discussion of Cambodian independence movements.

**Evening:** Meeting with a representative from a local human rights organization.

## Day 4: Contemporary Cambodia

**Morning:** Visit to a local university for discussion with Cambodian students about their perspectives on the future.

**Afternoon:** Tour of contemporary art spaces, exploring how artists process historical memory.

**Evening:** Free time for independent exploration.

## Day 5: Renewal and Challenges

**Morning:** Visit to a local NGO working in education or community development.

**Afternoon:** Final reflection session and departure.

## Learning Outcomes

- Deep understanding of Cambodia's 20th-century history
- Critical engagement with genocide and its aftermath
- Appreciation for Cambodia's contemporary challenges and achievements
- Personal connection with Cambodian perspectives
